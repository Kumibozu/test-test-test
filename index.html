Collapse of the Era of the Globe

c. 0–120 A.C. (After Collapse)

Fall of the Global Concord

The last remnants of the Era of the Globe fracture under resource wars and ideological schisms. City‑states retreat behind walls, and the myth of a unified world becomes a cautionary tale.

Fragmentation of the Continental Councils

Once‑cooperative councils turn inward, hoarding archives and weaponry. The first proto‑Commonwealth ideas are whispered in underground salons.

Era of Anarchy

c. 120–210 A.C.

Warlord Cartels of the Gap

Merchant houses in the Gap weaponize trade routes, taxing famine and selling safety. Their ledgers become as feared as any sword.

Urban Tithe Riots

In the crumbling cities of Drezbaeryn, the Urban Tithe sparks riots as citizens refuse to fund absentee governors and private militias.

Rise of the Khynmour Republic

c. 210–260 A.C.

Khynmour Civic Charter

A coalition of guilds, scholars, and ex‑officers draft the Khynmour Charter, promising representation in exchange for service and tax discipline.

Montague’s First Pamphlets

A minor clerk named Montague publishes anonymous tracts arguing that sovereignty belongs to the governed, not the garrison.

Formation of the Commonwealth

c. 260–320 A.C.

Revolutionary Commonwealth of Montague

Montague’s movement seizes the capital, replacing hereditary councils with elected assemblies. The Commonwealth’s red‑and‑gold sigil becomes a symbol of both hope and terror.

Codification of the Urban Tithe

The Tithe is re‑framed as a civic duty, funding public works and secret police in equal measure.

Indros Industrialisation

c. 320–380 A.C.

Steam‑Forges of Indros

Indros harnesses river‑heat and coal seams, birthing foundries that can arm ten armies at once. Its militias become the steel backbone of regional conflicts.

Mi’yam Relic Excavations

Deep beneath Indros, Mi’yam relics are unearthed—devices that blur the line between technology and theology. Every faction wants them.

Gap Pass Campaign

c. 380–410 A.C.

Siege of the Gap Citadels

Commonwealth legions and Indros militias attempt to break the merchant houses’ chokehold on the mountain passes. The campaign becomes a ledger of atrocities.

Wifuni Zulun City War

In the river‑cities of Wifuni Zulun, proxy forces clash over tariffs and relic smuggling. The war is never formally declared, only accounted.

Rise of Ryenarkia

c. 410–460 A.C.

Coronation of Darwues the Emperor

Darwues unites the Ryenarkian heartlands under an imperial cult, claiming divine mandate through Halphadite prophecy and battlefield miracles.

Ryenarkian Imperial Cult Edicts

The Imperial Cult rewrites law, calendar, and ritual. Loyalty to Darwues becomes both civic duty and sacrament.

Modern Cold War

c. 460 A.C. – Present

Commonwealth–Ryenarkia Cold War

The Commonwealth and Ryenarkia avoid open war, instead funding revolutions, militias, and cults across Drezbaeryn. Every border becomes a fault line.

Shadow Archives of Drezbaeryn

Historians, spies, and priests compete to control the narrative. The truth of the past becomes the most valuable weapon of the present.

Cold War Dashboard

A high‑level view of the ideological struggle between the Commonwealth and Ryenarkia.

Commonwealth Sphere

  • Ideology: Revolutionary republicanism, civic duty, controlled markets.
  • Methods: Funding assemblies, arming militias, exporting charters and pamphlets.
  • Influence Zones: Khynmour, Indros industrial councils, urban guilds of Drezbaeryn.
  • Proxy Tools: Revolutionary Commonwealth of Montague cells, trade unions, civic militias.

Ryenarkian Sphere

  • Ideology: Imperial cult, divine mandate, hierarchical order.
  • Methods: Sponsoring cults, backing monarchist restorations, blessing loyal warlords.
  • Influence Zones: Ryenarkian heartlands, Halphadite shrines, frontier garrisons.
  • Proxy Tools: Ryenarkian Imperial Cult, temple militias, loyalist city councils.

Global Tension

Officially, peace. Unofficially, every border is a battlefield of influence, relics, and narratives.
